What is ColoSensus ?

ColoSensus is a web-application integrated with a machine learning model. It is mainly designed and trained to predict and detect a healthy colon and colons with indicators of colorectal cancer, ulcerative colitis, and colon polyps. Colosensus only takes in colonoscopy videos instead of images, reducing the burden of saving key frames from the users' end.

How does ColoSensus predict diseases?

ColoSensus' model harnesses the power of convolutional neural networks to interpret videos frame by frame. This type of neural network gathers information from features standing out per frame and gathers more from the temporal (or time) aspect to finally predict whether the given colonoscopy video shows a healthy colon or a colon with manifestations of colorectal cancer, ulcerative colitis, or colon polyps.

How accurate is ColoSensus 's prediction?

ColoSensus's model was evaluated using an unseen dataset, meaning it relied on the information it gathered during training. It boasted an 82% accuracy, 87% precision, 82% recall, and achieved an F1-Score of 79%. Moreover, the model learned on several cycles of training to ensure it captured the most complex patterns found on each case of colon disease.
